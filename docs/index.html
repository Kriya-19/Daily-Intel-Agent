🔥 Contrarian Take

The current AI 'gold rush,' marked by aggressive investment and rapid deployment, masks significant underlying operational and strategic fragilities. Instead of continuous, unchecked growth, the industry may be headed for a period of painful rationalization, where companies re-evaluate unsustainable compute costs, address critical security flaws, and struggle to integrate unreliable AI agents into core business processes.
Multiple reports highlight that enterprises are buying AI infrastructure faster than they can measure costs, experiencing frequent security incidents with AI agents, lacking trust in agent outputs, and shipping under-evaluated agents to production. OpenAI's accidental breach and Google's uncertain model release strategy further underscore the immaturity and risks of current AI deployment, suggesting a correction is more likely than unbridled success.
