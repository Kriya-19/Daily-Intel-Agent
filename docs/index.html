🔥 Contrarian Take

Despite the overwhelming narrative of a $1 trillion AI supercycle and quantum computing nearing commercial viability, history suggests that even transformative technologies are susceptible to speculative bubbles. The current enthusiasm and massive capital inflows might reflect 'popular delusions' that could lead to corrections, regardless of underlying technological merit.
Edward Thorp's quote emphasizes that 'financial irrationalities' predate modern technology, while the historical account of 'Memoirs of Extraordinary Popular Delusions and the Madness of Crowds' highlights how human psychology often drives market extremes. The rapid escalation of AI investments and valuations could be entering such a phase, where exuberance may outpace fundamental growth in the short to medium term.
