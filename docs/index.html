🔥 Contrarian Take

Despite the prevailing narrative of a rapid, transformative AI boom, the practical deployment and widespread adoption of advanced AI agents are proving slower and more challenging than often portrayed. This suggests a disconnect between aspirational timelines and the current reality of technology development.
Mark Zuckerberg's candid admission that AI agents haven't progressed as quickly as hoped directly contrasts with the constant stream of new AI product launches and funding announcements. The emergence of free alternatives to costly AI coding tools like Claude Code (e.g., Goose) further indicates that cost and perceived value are still significant barriers to mass adoption for even powerful AI applications. The 'AI hype' evident in a sandwich shop's IPO documents also points to market enthusiasm outpacing tangible, broad-based application readiness.
